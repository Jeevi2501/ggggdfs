Timeline of Scientific Discoveries

Ancient Discoveries Modern Discoveries Recent Discoveries

Ancient Discoveries

  • Wheel (around 3500 BC) - Invented in Mesopotamia, it transformed transportation and engineering.
  • Fire (Prehistoric) - Early humans discovered how to control fire, leading to cooking, warmth, and protection.
  • Pythagoras' Theorem (around 500 BC) - Ancient Greeks discovered fundamental principles in mathematics.
  • Heliocentric Theory (1543) - Nicolaus Copernicus proposed that the Earth revolves around the Sun.

Modern Discoveries (16th to 20th Century)

  • Gravity (1687) - Isaac Newton formulated the law of universal gravitation, explaining the force of attraction between masses.
  • Electricity (18th Century) - Benjamin Franklin and others made foundational discoveries about electricity.
  • Germ Theory (1861) - Louis Pasteur discovered that microorganisms cause disease, revolutionizing medicine.
  • Penicillin (1928) - Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin, leading to the development of antibiotics.
  • DNA Structure (1953) - James Watson, Francis Crick, and Rosalind Franklin discovered the double helix structure of DNA.

Recent Discoveries (21st Century)

  • Higgs Boson (2012) - Scientists at CERN discovered the Higgs boson particle, which helps explain why particles have mass.
  • Gravitational Waves (2015) - LIGO detected gravitational waves, confirming a major prediction of Einstein's theory of relativity.
  • CRISPR Gene Editing (2012) - This revolutionary tool allows scientists to edit genes with unprecedented precision.
  • First Image of a Black Hole (2019) - The Event Horizon Telescope captured the first image of a black hole.
